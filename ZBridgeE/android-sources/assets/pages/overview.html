Overview.

Bridge is a game played by four people divided into two teams.  The players sit around a table with the teammates (also called partners) sitting across the table facing each other.  The four player positions are assigned the compass positions of North, East, South, and West.  Note that the designations are usually relative to each observer; i.e., from your vantage point as a player you would usually consider yourself South, looking across at your partner in the North position.

Each hand of Bridge consists of two stages, Bidding and Card Play.  The bidding stage (also called the auction) occurs first, followed by the card play.  One or more hands are played in a single game, and two or three games are played in a match, called a rubber.
