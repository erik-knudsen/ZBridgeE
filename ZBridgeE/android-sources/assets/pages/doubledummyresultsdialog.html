Double Dummy Results

Double dummy play is a play where all cards are visible to all players. Statistical studies have shown that the overall picture of double dummy play is that actual declarer play does slightly better than double-dummy play except at the slam level where double-dummy play has a slight edge. The reason is apparent: The opening lead gives the defense a considerable advantage at double-dummy; e.g., the perfect lead will sometimes bury a contract before declarer has a chance. As the contract level increases, the opening lead tends to be less crucial, so the advantage tapers off, disappearing entirely from 5 NT upward.
