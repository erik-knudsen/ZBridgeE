Game Options.

Interface.


Here you can choose if you want a pause between bids and/or you want a pause between plays. And you choose how many seconds there should be between bids/plays.


Scoring.


The Scoring dialog allows you to choose the mode of the bridge play when you play with cards dealt by the computer i.e. not from a PBN file. When cards are dealt from a PBN file the scoring mode is given in the file.

  • Teams IMP.
    You play the cards with the computer in one play and the computer plays the cards in another play (simultaneously). The two plays are then given scores and compared with International Match Point (IMP).

  • Duplicate MP.
    Is the same as Teams IMP except the comparison are with Match Point (MP).

  • Rubber Bridge.
    A rubber bridge play can be played.

  • Practice.
    You just play the cards given. Scores are calculated, but no comparison is done.

Level.


The Level dialog allows you to choose the level of computer processing for the computer player in the play of cards. The processing level affects the number of double dummy plays the computer investigages for calculating the best card to play next. The higher the level the better the choice of the best card will be.

  • Level 0.
    The lowest level. For computers with not much processing power.

  • Level 1.
    Intermediate level.

  • Level 2.
    Intermediate level.

  • Level 3.
    The highest level.
