Computer Player Logic.

The computer plays much better than novice players, though not as well as expert bridge players. Bidding and playing is done naturally, just like an experienced player.


Bidding Rule Tables.

Bid algorithms are based on Bidding Rule Tables. The bid goes first through the Bidding Rule Tables and thereafter the computer bids following a built-in approach. The Bidding Rule Tables are characteristic for the the bidding system selected: four- or five-card majors etc. ZBridge has many bidding options to choose among.


Random generated hands.

Play algorithms are based on random generated hands. The computer generates unknown hands randomly with constraints base on information from the auction (HCP range, suit length, number of Aces, etc.). The computer calculates best play based on a double dummy algorithm. A number of simulation trials are performed and afterwards the computer chooses among these best plays using  information based on the defenders' signals and finally makes the final play.

Built-In Approach of ZBridge Bidding.

ZBridge uses the Bidding Rule Tables in the early stage of the auction. After the bid goes through the Bidding Rule Tables, the computer bids according to the built-in approach. Built-in approaches are as follows.


Non-Forcing and Limit.

New suit bids and rebids are usually non-forcing. Notrump bids and raises of partner's suit are limit bids.


Cue Bids.

Guarantees added strength to bid an opponent's suit.


Exception.

After a minor suit fit has been found, a new suit bid of three normally shows stoppers in the bid suit.


Doubles.

doubles are for penalties, when the partner shows strength. Otherwise, doubles against three or lower bids are for takeout and doubles against four or higher bids are for penalties.


Ace Asking Bids.

4NT is usually a Blackwood convention. 4C after 1NT or 2NT opening bids is Gerber convention.


Hand Valuation of ZBridge.

ZBridge uses the hand valuation shown next:

High Card Points (HCP)
Ace=4    King=3     Queen=2    Jack=1

Distribution Points (DP) when a trump fit is found
1 point for each doubleton
2 points for each singleton
3 points for each void

Long Suit Points (LP)
Long suits have a value beyond the HCP held. Add one point for each card exceeding 4 cards in a suit.

Trump Fit Points (FP)
Add 1 point for each trump exceeding 8-card fitting.

Total Points (TP)
When intending to make a bid in a suit and there is not agreement on trump suit, add high card points (HCP) and long suit points (LP) to get the total point value of one's hand. When intending to raise an agreed trump suit, add high card points (HCP) and distribution points (DP) and trump fit points (FP). When making a bid in notrump with intent to play, value high card points (HCP) only.

Control Number
Ace=2    King=1

Required Points for a Game and Slam Contract

4 of a suit 26 total points
5 of a suit 29 total points
6 of a suit 33 total points.
7 of a sui 37 total points.
3NT 26 total points.
6NT 33 total points.
7NT 37 total points.


Point Ranges
ZBridge bids according to the following point ranges.

Opener

Minimum 12-15
Intermediate 16-18 (game invitation)
Strong 19-21 (game forcing)
Very Strong (strong two opening)


Responder
Minimum 6-9
Intermediate 10-12 (game invitation)
Strong 13-15 (game forcing)
Very Strong (jump shift)


Note:
ZBridge jumps to a new suit with 16 or more points after the partner opens with one in a suit.
